Introduction

This is the companion webpage of the aforementioned paper, submitted to the 13th DAFx Conference. It contains the source-signals, the extracted tracks, and complementary information on the work reported in the paper. The webpage contains information about the source-signals and the extracted tracks.
A zip file containing all source-signals and extracted tracks can be downloaded here (large file, 32.5 MB).

1. Source-signals

The source signals in the database are grouped in levels of increasing content complexity (see Section 3.1 of the paper). With the exception of the signals at Level 0, the source-signals are excerpts of the Real World Computing Database (RWC). 2. Partial Tracks

The obtained tracks for each source-signals are stored in a Matlab® .mat file compatible with Matlab® version 7 and above. Each track is stored in a data structure with 4 fields:
  • onset - a scalar denoting the estimated (in seconds) onset time of the track
  • freq - a vector containing the estimated (in Hz) frequency trajectory  of the track
  • amp - a vector containing the estimated (in absolute values) amplitude trajectory of the track
  • phase - a vector containing the estimated (in radians) phase trajectory of the track
All trajectories are sampled at 500 Hz, with parameters represented in floating-point format with double precision.

In the tables below, one finds links to download the partial tracks, as well as related information. Signals synthesized (the method described here has been used) from the obtained tracks can also be downloaded for Levels 1 to 3. The synthesized signals tend to have a smoothed onset (when compared to source-signals), an effect inherent to the sinusoidal model.

As an example of the methodology employed, the script used to obtain the tracks of the Accordion signal at Level 1 can be download here. The scripts for the remaining signals can be made available upon request.
